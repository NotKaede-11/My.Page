Bachelor of Science in Computer Science

Is a four-year undergraduate degree that focuses on the technical and scientific aspects of computing. The curriculum emphasizes mathematical and theoretical foundations, alongside practical applications in areas such as programming, data structures, operating systems, computer architecture, and the theory of computation. Students can also specialize in fields like artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, or software engineering, depending on the institution. Through this program, students develop technical skills in programming and software development, as well as analytical and soft skills essential for problem-solving and teamwork. Graduates are equipped for various roles, including software developer, systems analyst, web developer, and data scientist.

Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) with a specialization in Data Science

Prepares students to analyze, interpret, and derive insights from large data sets. The program combines core computer science principles with specialized courses such as data mining, machine learning, and big data technologies. Students gain practical experience through hands-on projects that apply these techniques to real-world challenges. Graduates develop skills in data analysis, statistical methods, programming in languages like Python and R, and problem-solving. Career opportunities include roles such as data scientist, data analyst, machine learning engineer, and data engineer.

PHILOSOPHY

I chose to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science because of my passion for web development and programming. The idea of creating websites and building functional systems excites me, and I believe that learning how to program is not just a valuable skill but also a fun and fulfilling experience. The more I dive into the complexities of coding, the more I feel like I’m not just learning but building the future I want for myself.

What motivates me the most is the sense of accomplishment I get after completing a task, no matter how big or small. Each project feels like a significant achievement, and the feeling of learning something new is one of the greatest rewards in life. It pushes me to keep going, knowing that every step forward brings me closer to mastery.

My ultimate goal is to become a successful web developer, but I don’t want to stop there. I envision myself creating my own programs—whether it's a chess engine, AI, chatbot, or even my own games. I want to expand my knowledge and skills in a way that allows me to bring my ideas to life.

When it comes to learning, I believe in taking things slowly. I prefer to break down complex topics into manageable parts, making it easier for me to understand and retain information. I also embrace trial and error because mistakes are an essential part of the learning process. This approach helps me grow, not just as a student, but as a problem-solver.

Technology plays a huge role in shaping my perspective. It provides me with instant access to information and solutions to the challenges I face. It also helps me stay connected with the people I care about. Ultimately, technology is the reason I chose Computer Science, as it opens up endless possibilities for creativity, problem-solving, and connection.
